# VendoorProof Privacy Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** 13 July 2026

## 1. Key information

This Privacy Policy explains how we process personal data in connection with:

- the use of **vendoorproof.com** and its language versions;
- contacting us, submitting an enquiry, joining a pilot, waitlist or newsletter;
- creating and managing a VendoorProof account;
- using the VendoorProof application to collect, route, track and follow up on vendor documents;
- technical support, security and billing.

VendoorProof supports document workflow and follow-up automation. It does not provide legal, insurance or compliance advice and does not decide on a user's behalf whether a document satisfies contractual or regulatory requirements. Assessments requiring judgement remain subject to review by an authorised person.

Using the website does not constitute consent to all processing. Where consent is required, we request it separately and allow it to be withdrawn later.

## 3. When VendoorProof is a controller and when it is a processor

### 3.1. VendoorProof as controller

We act as controller in relation to, among other things, personal data concerning:

- website visitors;
- people who contact us;
- people joining a pilot, waitlist or newsletter;
- customers, prospective customers, account users and customer representatives;
- people contacting technical support;
- information required for billing, security and the establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims.

### 3.2. VendoorProof as processor

Where a customer uploads or otherwise provides personal data concerning its vendors, employees, contractors or supplier representatives, the customer will generally determine the purposes and means of that processing and remain the controller. VendoorProof processes such data on the customer's behalf as a processor under a data processing agreement.

This may include:

- vendor representative contact details;
- message content and follow-up history;
- document metadata;
- personal data contained in uploaded documents;
- statuses, notes and human-review outcomes.

For matters relating to data uploaded to VendoorProof by a particular customer, the relevant individual should normally contact that customer first. We will assist the customer with data subject requests in accordance with our agreement and applicable law.

This Privacy Policy does not replace the data processing agreement entered into with a customer.

## 4. Personal data we may process

Depending on how VendoorProof is used, we may process the following categories of data.

### 4.1. Contact and professional data

- first and last name;
- business email address and telephone number;
- job title, department and organisation;
- enquiry, pilot application or correspondence content.

### 4.2. Account and organisation data

- user identifier;
- organisation name and role within the organisation;
- account settings, language and preferences;
- login and application activity history;
- permissions assigned to the user.

We do not store passwords in plain text. Where authentication is provided by an external identity provider, we receive only the information required to create a session and administer the account.

### 4.3. Vendor document workflow data

- vendor name and representative details;
- document type, status, expiry date and metadata;
- reminder messages, communications and notes;
- documents uploaded by a user or vendor;
- extracted data, labels, flags and human-review outcomes.

Customers should limit uploaded information to what is necessary for the relevant workflow. Special category data, criminal conviction data, full identity document numbers and other highly sensitive information should not be uploaded unless this is genuinely necessary, lawful and expressly permitted under the agreement with VendoorProof.

### 4.4. Technical and usage data

- IP address;
- request date and time;
- browser, operating system and device type;
- session identifiers;
- pages visited and technical events;
- error, security and diagnostic logs;
- approximate location derived from an IP address, such as country or region.

### 4.5. Billing data

If the service is paid, we may process data required to enter into and perform the contract, issue accounting documents and administer payments. Payment card details should be processed directly by the payment provider, and VendoorProof should not receive full card details.

## 5. Sources of personal data

We may obtain personal data:

- directly from the individual;
- from the individual's employer or organisation that creates an account for them;
- from a customer using VendoorProof;
- through correspondence, forms and meetings relating to a pilot or sale;
- from the browser, device and technical infrastructure while the website or application is used;
- from authentication, payment, hosting and other supporting service providers, to the extent required to provide the service.

Where a vendor representative's data has been entered into the application by a customer, the source is that customer or one of its authorised users.

Before relying on legitimate interests, we assess whether those interests are overridden by the interests, rights or freedoms of the individual.

## 7. Whether providing data is mandatory

Providing personal data is generally voluntary. However, certain information is required to:

- answer an enquiry;
- take part in a pilot;
- create an account;
- enter into and perform a contract;
- process payment or comply with accounting obligations;
- keep the service secure.

Failure to provide required information may prevent us from fulfilling the relevant purpose.

## 8. Automation and decision-making

VendoorProof may automatically:

- schedule and send reminders;
- organise documents and metadata;
- identify missing fields or upcoming expiry dates;
- apply technical statuses;
- route an item to human review or escalation.

We do not use these operations to make decisions based solely on automated processing that produce legal effects concerning an individual or similarly significantly affect that individual. Final compliance assessments, document acceptance and business decisions remain with the customer and its authorised users.

## 11. Retention periods

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected and thereafter for any period required by law or needed to establish, exercise or defend legal claims.

As a general rule:

- account and contract data is retained for the duration of the agreement and afterwards for applicable legal obligations and limitation periods;
- accounting records are retained for the period required under tax and accounting laws;
- pilot, waitlist or newsletter data is retained until consent is withdrawn, a valid objection is received or the purpose is no longer current, but no longer than **24 months after the last meaningful interaction**, unless a contractual relationship begins;
- correspondence and support requests are retained for the time required to resolve the matter and generally for up to **24 months** afterwards, unless a longer period is needed for legal obligations or claims;
- security and diagnostic logs are retained for a period proportionate to the risk, normally no longer than **90 days**, unless needed to investigate an incident;
- customer data processed on the customer's behalf is retained in accordance with the agreement and, after termination, returned or deleted on the customer's instructions; data in rotating backups is deleted at the end of the backup retention cycle, no later than **[[BACKUP_RETENTION_DAYS]] days**.

Data may be kept for longer where required by law, a binding authority request, preservation of evidence or an ongoing dispute.
